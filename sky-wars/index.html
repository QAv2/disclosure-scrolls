Act II

The History

Weather modification is not theoretical. Governments have been doing it since 1946. Here are the programs they don’t teach in school.

The Programs

Vincent Schaefer
GE researcher — first cloud-seeding flight, 1946
USAF
Funded every major U.S. weather mod program
Documented
Project Cirrus (1946): Vincent Schaefer at GE, funded by the Army Signal Corps, conducted the first cloud-seeding flight. In October 1947, 80 kg of dry ice was dropped into a hurricane — which reversed course and struck Georgia, causing $2 million in damage. Project Stormfury (1962–1983): NOAA/Navy program to weaken hurricanes with silver iodide. 21 years of operation. Hurricane Debbie (1969) saw a 31% wind speed reduction. Project Cumulus (UK, 1949): RAF cloud seeding over England. On August 16, 1952, 230mm of rain fell on Lynmouth in 24 hours, killing 34 people.
Earth Magazine, “Benchmarks: Disaster of Project Cirrus.” NOAA AOML, Project Stormfury. BBC, Project Cumulus documentary (2001).

Three countries. Three decades. Hurricanes redirected, monsoons intensified, floods triggered. And that was just the beginning.

Operation Popeye (1967–1972)

This is the one that changes everything.

Documented
The U.S. Air Force ran a classified cloud-seeding campaign over the Ho Chi Minh Trail to extend monsoon season by 30–45 days. WC-130 aircraft flew from Thailand, dispersing silver iodide and lead iodide into the clouds above Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The motto: “Make mud, not war.”
State Department FRUS, frus1964-68v28/d274 (declassified). Popular Science, “Operation Popeye.”

The ENMOD Treaty (1977)

Documented
The Convention on the Prohibition of Military or Any Other Hostile Use of Environmental Modification Techniques. 78 state parties. The U.S. ratified in 1980. Bans weather modification for hostile purposes with “widespread, long-lasting or severe effects.” Does NOT address non-hostile uses. Does NOT require disclosure. Does NOT cover “climate remediation.” The treaty banned the weapon. It said nothing about everything else.
UN Office for Disarmament Affairs — ENMOD Convention.

The treaty addressed the war. It left the sky wide open.

And everyone else kept going.

Documented
The Soviet Union began weather modification in the 1930s. By 1976, hail suppression covered ~5 million hectares using 70mm artillery firing silver iodide projectiles. Russia’s program has now run continuously for 65+ years. The CIA documented Soviet cloud physics programs in declassified reports. China now operates the world’s largest program with 37,000+ employees. The UAE runs ~300 cloud-seeding missions per year with AI-powered autonomous cloud analysis.
CIA FOIA document 06541158. China State Council, December 2020. Gulf News, “UAE Cloud Seeding 2025.”

The U.S. weaponized weather in a war zone, got caught, and the world passed a treaty to stop it. The treaty banned the weapon. It didn’t ban the technology. It didn’t even require anyone to tell you they were using it.

The Patents

A defense contractor sat down and designed a system to spray aluminum oxide in the stratosphere. Then they patented it. Then Raytheon bought it.

Patents don’t prove deployment. But they prove intent, capability, and ownership.

The Welsbach Patent (1991)

Hughes Aircraft
Filed the patent — now Raytheon
Raytheon
Acquired Hughes — current owner
Documented
US Patent 5,003,186 — “Stratospheric Welsbach Seeding for Reduction of Global Warming.” Proposes dispersing particles of aluminum oxide and thorium oxide into the stratosphere at 7–13 km altitude to convert trapped infrared radiation. Particle size: 10–100 microns. One proposed delivery method: “via the jet fuel.” Particles “may remain in suspension for up to one year.”
Google Patents — US 5,003,186. Inventors: David B. Chang, I-Fu Shih. Filed 1990, granted 1991.

Was it deployed? No evidence. The patent expired without documented test flights or government contracts. The citation chain is thin — only a handful of forward citations in 35 years. The inventors were IR/optics engineers, not atmospheric scientists. This is not the footprint of an active deployment program.

But it exists. A defense contractor patented stratospheric metal-oxide spraying. And the patent is now owned by Raytheon. That is a fact you can verify on Google Patents right now.

Bernard Eastlund and the HAARP Patent

Bernard J. Eastlund
Physicist — HAARP patent inventor
HAARP
3.6 MW ionospheric research facility, Alaska

Bernard J. Eastlund was a physicist working for ARCO — the Atlantic Richfield Company, an oil giant. In 1985, he filed a patent through ARCO’s subsidiary APTI for a method of altering the ionosphere using a ground-based antenna array. The listed applications included disrupting missiles, interfering with communications, and altering upper-atmosphere wind patterns. Eastlund died in 2007. His patent lived on — traveling through a chain of defense contractors.

Documented
US Patent 4,686,605 — The “HAARP patent.” The patent specifies 1–100 GW of power. HAARP operates at ~3.6 MW — roughly 300 to 30,000 times less. HAARP program manager John Heckscher stated: “HAARP certainly does not have anything to do with Eastlund’s thing.” Whether or not HAARP implements Eastlund’s vision, the patent chain tells its own story.
Google Patents — US 4,686,605. Filed 1985, granted 1987. APTI, Inc. (now BAE Systems).

And a 1974 patent (US 3,813,875) literally describes a rocket system for releasing barium into the atmosphere to create ion clouds.

Documented
