Act II

The History

Weather modification is not theoretical. Governments have been doing it since 1946. The history is declassified, published, and available to anyone willing to read it.

Project Cirrus (1946)

Vincent Schaefer
GE researcher — first cloud-seeding flight
USAF
Army Signal Corps funding
Documented
The first U.S. government weather modification program. Vincent Schaefer at General Electric, funded by the Army Signal Corps, conducted the first cloud-seeding flight. In October 1947, 80 kg of dry ice was dropped into a hurricane — which then reversed course and struck Georgia, causing $2 million in damage. The era of human weather modification had begun.
Earth Magazine, “Benchmarks: October 13, 1947 — Disaster of Project Cirrus.”

Project Stormfury (1962–1983)

Documented
A joint NOAA/Navy program to weaken hurricanes by seeding them with silver iodide. Hurricane Debbie (1969) showed a 31% wind speed reduction after seeding. The program ran for 21 years before being cancelled when scientists realized natural hurricane variability couldn’t be distinguished from the intervention. Two decades of trying to control hurricanes.
NOAA Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory, Project Stormfury documentation.

Operation Popeye (1967–1972)

This is the big one.

USAF
2,600+ cloud-seeding sorties
Documented
The U.S. Air Force ran a classified cloud-seeding campaign over the Ho Chi Minh Trail to extend monsoon season by 30–45 days. Over 2,600 sorties were flown from Thailand using silver iodide and lead iodide. Cost: ~$3.6 million per year. Intelligence confirmed 30–45% precipitation increases in targeted areas. The motto: “Make mud, not war.”
State Department FRUS — Declassified Documents, frus1964-68v28/d274. Popular Science, “Operation Popeye.”
Documented
It was classified. It was exposed by journalist Jack Anderson in 1971. It was confirmed in Senate hearings in 1974. The U.S. government weaponized weather, got caught, and the world passed a treaty to stop it. That’s not a theory.
Senate hearings, 1974. Congressional Record. Wikipedia — Operation Popeye (sourced from declassified records).

The United States weaponized weather in a war zone. It was classified, exposed, confirmed under oath, and caused an international treaty. That is not disputed — it is in the Congressional Record.

The ENMOD Treaty (1977)

Documented
The Convention on the Prohibition of Military or Any Other Hostile Use of Environmental Modification Techniques. 78 state parties. The U.S. ratified in 1980. Bans weather modification for hostile purposes with “widespread, long-lasting or severe effects.” Does NOT address non-hostile uses — a gap that matters enormously today.
UN Office for Disarmament Affairs — ENMOD Convention.

The Global Scale

Documented
The Soviet Union began weather modification in the 1930s. By 1976, hail suppression covered ~5 million hectares. Methods: 70mm artillery firing silver iodide projectiles, An-30M “Sky Cleaner” aircraft, ground-launched rockets. Russia’s operational hail suppression program has now run continuously for 65+ years. The CIA documented Soviet cloud physics programs in declassified reports.
CIA FOIA — Soviet Weather Modification (document 06541158). ResearchGate — “Results of 65-Years Project of Hail Suppression in Russian Federation.”

Act III

The Patents

Patents don’t prove deployment. But they prove that serious organizations — including defense contractors — have designed, documented, and filed intellectual property for atmospheric modification technologies.

The Welsbach Patent (1991)

Hughes Aircraft
Now Raytheon
Raytheon
Current patent holder
Documented
US Patent 5,003,186 — “Stratospheric Welsbach Seeding for Reduction of Global Warming.” Filed by Hughes Aircraft Company (now Raytheon). Proposes dispersing tiny particles of aluminum oxide and thorium oxide into the stratosphere at 7–13 km altitude. The particles would convert trapped infrared radiation into far-infrared radiation that escapes to space. Particle size: 10–100 microns. One proposed delivery method: “via the jet fuel.” Particles “may remain in suspension for up to one year.”
Google Patents — US 5,003,186. Inventors: David B. Chang, I-Fu Shih. Filed 1990, granted 1991.
Documented
Was it deployed? No evidence of operational deployment. The patent expired without documented test flights, pilot programs, or government contracts. The citation chain is remarkably thin — only a handful of forward citations in 35 years, suggesting no substantial follow-on R&D. The inventors were IR/optics engineers, not atmospheric scientists. But it exists. A defense contractor patented spraying aluminum oxide in the stratosphere. And it’s now owned by Raytheon.
Google Patents citation chain analysis. Metabunk — Welsbach Patent Analysis.

The Eastlund Patent (1987)

Bernard J. Eastlund
HAARP patent inventor
HAARP
High-freq Active Auroral Research
Documented
US Patent 4,686,605 — “Method and Apparatus for Altering a Region in the Earth’s Atmosphere.” The “HAARP patent.” Describes using a ground-based antenna array to transmit electromagnetic radiation upward, exciting electron cyclotron resonance in the ionosphere. Listed applications: disrupting missiles/aircraft, interfering with communications, and altering upper-atmosphere wind patterns. The power problem: the patent specifies 1–100 GW. HAARP operates at ~3.6 MW — roughly 300 to 30,000 times less.
Google Patents — US 4,686,605. Filed 1985, granted 1987. APTI, Inc. (now BAE Systems).

Act VI

The Sky Is Open

If you want to be taken seriously on this topic, you have to be honest about what the evidence does and doesn’t support.

The Scientific Consensus

Documented
A 2016 study published in Environmental Research Letters, led by Carnegie’s Ken Caldeira, surveyed 77 atmospheric chemists and geochemists. 76 of 77 (98.7%) said they had NOT encountered evidence of a secret large-scale atmospheric program. The one dissenting expert cited unusually high levels of atmospheric barium in a remote area. All 77 agreed the cited evidence could be explained by normal contrail formation and poor sampling methods.
University of California, “Surveyed Scientists Debunk Chemtrails Conspiracy Theory,” 2016. Environmental Research Letters.
Documented
Contrail persistence is a well-understood phenomenon. Jet exhaust mixes with cold upper-atmosphere air; ice crystals form on soot nuclei. Whether a contrail dissipates quickly or persists for hours depends entirely on ambient humidity and temperature. Long-lasting contrails have been documented since World War II. Contrail cirrus contributes more than half of aviation-induced radiative forcing and may triple by 2050.
Royal Aeronautical Society, “Contrails Explained.” Nature Communications, “Contrail Cirrus Climate Impact,” 2018.
