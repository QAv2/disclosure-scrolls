# THREAD 1: SKULL & BONES (ORDER 322)

## 1.1 Founding & Structure

**FACT**: Skull and Bones was founded in December 1832 at Yale University by William Huntington Russell and Alphonso Taft, after a dispute among Yale debating societies over Phi Beta Kappa awards.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Yale University archives; Britannica, "Skull and Bones" entry; Alexandra Robbins, *Secrets of the Tomb* (Little, Brown, 2002)

**FACT**: The number "322" in the society's insignia references 322 BC, the year of Greek orator Demosthenes' death. Members measure dates by adding 322 to the current year. A letter between early members in Yale's archives confirms this dating convention.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Yale University archival letter between early members; Robbins, *Secrets of the Tomb*

**FACT**: William Russell imported the society model from Germany, leading to the alternate interpretation that 322 means "'32, second chapter" of a German parent organization.
**TIER**: CREDIBLE
**SOURCE**: Antony Sutton, *America's Secret Establishment* (TrineDay, 2002); Robbins, *Secrets of the Tomb*

**FACT**: The Russell Trust Association is the legal and financial entity behind Skull and Bones. It was incorporated in 1856 by William Huntington Russell (president) and Daniel Coit Gilman (treasurer). The association holds all society assets, endowment, and property, including the "Tomb" (the society's 140+ year old clubhouse on Yale campus).
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Connecticut state incorporation records; Robbins, *Secrets of the Tomb*; Britannica

**FACT**: Fifteen juniors are "tapped" each year for membership. The society has initiated roughly 2,500 members since founding.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Yale records; New England Historical Society; Britannica

Key successor companies included Standard Oil of New Jersey (later Exxon), Standard Oil of New York (later Mobil), Standard Oil of California (later Chevron), Standard Oil of Indiana (later Amoco/BP). The Rockefeller family retained substantial ownership across the successor companies, meaning the breakup actually increased their net worth.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Supreme Court ruling 221 U.S. 1 (1911); Chernow, *Titan*; financial histories

**FACT**: David Rockefeller (1915-2017), grandson of John D., served as CEO of Chase Manhattan Bank from 1969-1981.

## 2.2 Dulles Brothers

**FACT**: Allen Welsh Dulles (1893-1969) and John Foster Dulles (1888-1959) were brothers who simultaneously held two of the most powerful positions in the U.S. government under President Eisenhower: Allen as Director of Central Intelligence (CIA Director, 1953-1961) and John Foster as Secretary of State (1953-1959).

**SOURCE**: Stephen Kinzer, *The Brothers: John Foster Dulles, Allen Dulles, and Their Secret World War* (Times Books, 2013); Britannica entries for both brothers; CIA official history

**FACT**: Both brothers were partners at Sullivan & Cromwell, one of the most powerful law firms on Wall Street. Sullivan & Cromwell's clients included I.G. Farben into an international nickel cartel, giving Nazi Germany access to the cartel's resources.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Kinzer, *The Brothers*; Peter Grose, *Gentleman Spy: The Life of Allen Dulles* (Houghton Mifflin, 1994); Nancy Lisagor and Frank Lipsius, *A Law Unto Itself: The Untold Story of the Law Firm Sullivan & Cromwell* (William Morrow, 1988)

**FACT**: Allen Dulles became the lawyer for the Thyssens' Rotterdam bank and represented multiple German industrial firms including I.G. Farben. **TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Kinzer, *The Brothers*; Grose, *Gentleman Spy*; declassified OSS files

**FACT**: Both brothers had direct connections to the United Fruit Company. John Foster Dulles was a former lawyer for United Fruit; Allen Dulles was formerly on United Fruit's Board of Directors. Sullivan & Cromwell was United Fruit's primary legal counsel.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Kinzer, *The Brothers*; Peter Chapman, *Bananas: How the United Fruit Company Shaped the World* (Canongate, 2007)

**FACT**: In 1954, Allen Dulles (CIA Director) and John Foster Dulles (Secretary of State) convinced Eisenhower to authorize Operation PBSUCCESS -- the CIA-backed overthrow of democratically elected Guatemalan President Jacobo Arbenz. **TIER**: DOCUMENTED

## 3.2 INSLAW/PROMIS Software

**FACT**: PROMIS (Prosecutor's Management Information System) was case management software developed by Inslaw, Inc., a Washington D.C.-based IT company founded by William A. Hamilton. Inslaw sued the Department of Justice in 1986, alleging the DOJ had stolen PROMIS through "trickery, fraud and deceit" by withholding contracted payments and then pirating the software.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Inslaw, Inc. v. United States, federal bankruptcy court; House Judiciary Committee report

**FACT**: In 1988, a federal bankruptcy judge ruled that the Department of Justice had indeed taken PROMIS software by "trickery, fraud, and deceit." This finding was upheld by a federal district court in 1988 but overturned on procedural grounds by the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals in 1991 (the appeals court did not rule on the merits, only on jurisdictional grounds -- whether the bankruptcy court had authority to hear the case).
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: Inslaw bankruptcy court ruling (1988); D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals ruling (1991); House Judiciary Committee report

**FACT**: In 1989, House Judiciary Committee Chairman Rep. Jack Brooks (D-TX) launched a three-year congressional investigation into the INSLAW matter. The resulting Brooks Committee report (dated August 11, 1992) concluded: "There appears to be strong evidence ... that the Department of Justice acted willfully and fraudulently, and took, converted and stole, INSLAW's Enhanced PROMIS by trickery, fraud and deceit."
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: House Judiciary Committee, "The INSLAW Affair: Investigative Report," 102nd Congress (August 11, 1992); available on govinfo.gov; Congressional Record, Volume 140, Issue 102, July 29, 1994

**FACT**: The Brooks report went further than the courts: "High government officials were involved. Individuals testified under oath that Inslaw's PROMIS software was stolen and distributed internationally in order to provide financial gain and to further intelligence and foreign policy objectives." Brooks recommended both a settlement for Inslaw and the appointment of a special prosecutor. Neither recommendation was implemented.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: House Judiciary Committee report (1992); Congressional Record

**FACT**: A separate DOJ investigation (the Bua Report, 1993) concluded there was "no credible evidence" supporting the conspiracy allegations. This directly contradicted the Brooks Committee's findings, creating an unresolved conflict between the legislative and executive branch investigations.
**TIER**: DOCUMENTED
**SOURCE**: DOJ press release, September 1994 ("New Report Finds No Credible Basis for INSLAW Case"); DOJ archives
